    <h1>Duties of RALC Committee Chairs </h1>
    <ol>
    	<li>Contact all committee members for correct email and phone numbers.  Update <a href="http://ralc.wikispot.org">the RALC 
        Wiki</a> with any new information.</li>
       	<li>Attend RALC Board Meetings held in January, April and October each year.  Take notes to present to your committee and 
        give a report to Directors of your committee’s activities.</li>
     	<li>Plan meetings:
         	<ol>
            	<li>Send announcements of meetings via email and also through Courier Service in print.  
                (If Directors receive a written meeting announcement at their Board meeting, more people attend.)</li>
         		<li>Minutes of your meetings should be approved by members (email is fine) and posted on the 
                <a href="http://ralc.wikispot.org">the RALC Wiki</a></li>
                <li>At the end of each meeting's agenda, it’s good to have sharing and question time.  Everyone learns from this.</li>
                <li>Review your committee's mission and revise as necessary.</li>
          </ol></li>
        <li>Through your committee, decide what program to sponsor.  RALC encourages one per committee per year.</li>
       	<li>Complete a Program Proposal and send to Paul Porterfield.  This form is on-line at the RALC Web page and easy to complete.</li>
       	<li>Send announcements to all committee members and Library Directors in plenty of time for planning.</li>
       	<li>Print the Program Evaluation Forms and have all participants complete these.  Turn in to Paul Porterfield.</li>
      	<li>After the program, complete the RALC Follow-up Program Report and present at the next Director’s Meeting. </li>
     </ol>
